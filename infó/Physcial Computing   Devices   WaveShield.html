Devices/WaveShield

Adafruit Wave Shield

Adafruit Wav shield web site
The Wave Shield is a unit that can play audio (specially formatted .wav) files - and it can be controlled by the arduino.

"Adding quality audio to an electronic project is surprisingly difficult. Here is a shield for Arduinos that solves this problem. It can play up to 22KHz, 12bit uncompressed audio files of any length. It's low cost, available as an easy-to-make kit. It has an onboard DAC, filter and op-amp for high quality output. Audio files are read off of an SD/MMC card, which are available at nearly any store. Volume can be controlled with the onboard thumbwheel potentiometer." Adafruit.com web site

What pins are used by the shield?

  • The Adafruit Wave Shield uses 2,3,4,5,10,11,12,13.
  • That means pins 6, 7, 8, 9 and the 6 analog in pins (also known as digital i/o pins 14-20) are available.

Is there any special format for the names of individual sound files?

  • Names have an limit of 8 characters and 3 extension characters e.g. "TRUMPETS.WAV"
    • No non-alphanumeric characters!

Are there any sensors or actuators that I CANNOT use with the Adafruit Wave Shield?

  • CapSense may not be usable as the Wave Shield uses most of the Arduino's processing power and resources.
  • Servos may also not work correctly for that same reason. (if you use Software Serial)
    * No PWM on PIN 9
    • "Timer 1 is used by the wave shield for timing.
  • see the Adafruit Wav shield FAQ for more info

What are my sound output choices for the Adafruit Wav Shield?

  • You can use the built-in headphone jack
  • You can build the MD amplifier board and connect it to Adafruit
  • solder in a female header so that you can output to a speaker.
